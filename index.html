Selected work

Five products, framed the way I think.

Each one started as a messy, high-stakes problem. The discipline is the same: find the real bottleneck, design the smallest thing that proves the bet, then scale it. Clients described by sector for confidentiality.

01 Global pharmaceutical organisation · AI / ML

ML-assisted clinical document authoring platform (0-to-1)

Problem

4,000+ medical, clinical and regulatory specialists authored compliance-critical documents through email and manual workflows. No fit-for-purpose product existed, and turnaround was slow and error-prone.

Approach

Set the product vision and MVP scope on IBM Watson — defining section priorities, ML task scope, confidence levels and acceptance thresholds. Core principle: automate the repetitive and pattern-heavy, flag uncertainty, keep human judgement on high-risk interpretation.

Outcome

80% faster turnaround (40h → 8h), ~$2M annual savings, launched at 80–90% model accuracy with a human-in-the-loop review architecture.

02 UK government agency · Public sector / GDS

Digital case-management service for animal housing (0-to-1)

Problem

Paper records, email approvals and manual reconciliation ran a high-volume regulated operation. Two very different user groups — field staff and admin — were forced through one unfit workflow.

Approach

Led a 12-person multidisciplinary team from discovery to adoption. Ran workshops to map the manual process, designed distinct flows for field and admin users, built on PowerApps to GDS/WCAG standards, and guided OCR for passport data capture.

Outcome

Case handling cut from 10 days to 2, 90% of manual processes automated, errors and backdating down 90%. Recognised with a promotion for leadership and impact.

GDS Service StandardWCAG 2.2 AAService designPowerAppsTeam leadership
03 UK tax authority · Enterprise platform

Valuation platform modernisation on Azure & Dynamics 365

Problem

Commercial tax valuation ran on brittle Excel and SQL legacy processes, serving 1,000+ valuers with slow, hard-to-maintain quality-control review.

Approach

Led product ownership for the QC workstream within the wider modernisation programme — refining backlogs across two cross-functional teams, translating complex processes into user stories and acceptance criteria, and driving low-code delivery on PowerApps.

Outcome

Operational redundancy reduced 30%, contributing to 80% efficiency gains and a 90% no-code solution rate across the workstream.

Product ownershipAzureDynamics 365Backlog & user storiesLow-code delivery
04 Leading UK health & beauty retailer · Data product

Customer data platform & analytics cloud migration

Problem

A customer data platform holding 20M+ records needed modernising before it could support unified profiles, segmentation and personalised campaigns.

Approach

Authored the modernisation roadmap, defined migration and transformation logic with structured validation, and built a data catalogue to surface redundancy and de-risk the move — laying the foundation for analytics-led marketing.

Outcome

Migration accuracy improved 25%, 15% redundant data identified to cut storage and migration cost, with retargeting workflows enabled across app and email.

05 Personal venture · GenAI prototype

Bille the Goat — a GenAI receipt-intelligence prototype

Problem

Consumer receipt management is fragmented across paper, email, downloads and apps — and most products fail at the very first step: capture.

Approach

Built a live prototype on Lovable to explore AI-native consumer design end to end: flexible ingestion channels, OCR extraction, natural-language search, budgeting, return-window and warranty reminders — connected to my writing on AI-mediated commerce.

Outcome

Evidence of venture-style product thinking at speed — spotting a pain point, shaping a proposition, and shipping a working prototype. Built for learning, not as a scaled product.
